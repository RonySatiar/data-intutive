NoPipeline

The NoPipeline concept champions for a flexible, long-term approach in data processing, moving away from traditional fixed or rigid pipeline structures.

It emphasizes adaptability and modularity, facilitating dynamic adjustments and experimentation. This approach is designed to be responsive to evolving scientific needs and technological advancements, ensuring easy integration and customization of data pipelines and tools as project demands evolve.

R&D Challenges: Innovate or Optimize ?

The world of biotech R&D is filled with paradoxes and complexities. There’s an overflowing wave of innovative biotechnology, but it’s counter-balanced by under-optimized IT. There’s a lack of standardization, leading to excessive R&D individualization that causes knowledge loss and disrupts productivity.

IT vs. Biology Focus

The overemphasis on managing complex IT reduces the time and energy spent on core biology research. This imbalance increases R&D costs, slows down discoveries, and impedes healthcare advancements.

Beyond the Pipeline

In the long term, innovation relies on more than just the computational workflow. For instance, the ability to find and reuse existing (organisational) knowledge plays a crucial role in driving innovative outcomes.

Discover agility in biotech research with NoPipeline's modular design – swiftly build your pipelines with this new methodologies. Embrace sustainability with reusable, enduring modules, minimizing redundancy while optimizing focus. Experience unparalleled efficiency and future-proof your research endeavors.

Viash Hub is the central platform in the NoPipeline approach, providing a well-documented repository for all modules and pipelines. It serves as a pivotal tool for managing computational knowledge, supporting managers, developers, and researchers alike. The platform fosters collaboration by enabling seamless search and sharing of knowledge, streamlining the research process.

Viash is the core tool of the NoPipeline approach, facilitating the creation of independently running modules for seamless pipeline construction and adaptation. Its innovative design distinctively separates operational aspects from scientific elements, empowering researchers to focus more intently on core research.

Embracing the NoPipeline Paradigm

Biotech R&D is evolving beyond traditional pipelines, Not Only the Pipeline is important! The NoPipeline approach, leveraging the Viash and the Viash Hub platform, caters to the diverse needs of the biotech community, from scientists to managers. This innovative strategy offers tactical and operational benefits, such as enhanced data processing efficiency, marking a significant shift in biotech data management. We will explore how NoPipeline challenges conventional methods, offering a more adaptable solution in the dynamic world of biotechnology.

Strategical

  • Modularity provides the ability to implement a long-term data workflow strategy for effectively managing and reusing knowledge.
  • A modular approach to data workflows leads to improved efficiency in the use of research staff and IT support, resulting in a higher-quality end product.
  • The use of code generation leads to standardized and validated high-quality software code and Nextflow modules to ensure the reliability and accuracy of biotech processes.
  • Using Viash prevents vendor lock-in(1):, ensuring long-term adaptability and sustainability for your R&D knowledge and organization.
The researcher created some very innovative and interesting tools, but after obtaining his Ph.D., he left the company, leaving it unclear where all the tools are and how to reuse them.
R&D Director

Tactical

  • Decoupling IT operations from research knowledge enables researchers to focus solely on scientific inquiries, free from complexities like reproducibility, scalability, and API management.
  • Viash simplifies the creation of Nextflow data pipelines by turning scripts into easily-integrated and individually executable modular building blocks.
  • Decoupling individual research tool development from pipeline development enhances reuse, improvement, and fosters efficient collaboration among researchers.
  • Viash’s modules facilitate flexible data pipeline design and development, allowing for easy adaptation to changing needs and enabling experimentation in a dynamic R&D environment.
Too much of my time is devoted to ensuring the IT technical operation of the data pipeline, whereas it would be more interesting to focus on the biological aspects and actual experimentation.
Computational Biologist R&D

Operational

  • Viash supports the combination of multiple scripting languages (Bash, Python, R, Scala, JS, C#) in a pipeline.
  • Viash generates standalone, Docker, and Nextﬂow executables to be run independently of the script’s language, using metadata as a basis.
  • Viash includes built-in unit testing capabilities for debugging and testing of individual components in a pipeline.
  • Viash executables include extra support for developers through command-line arguments, type checking, and validation of required parameters and input ﬁles.
